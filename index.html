About Us


Our Mission

Lyric Foundation Inc. is a 501(c)3 Nonprofit organization dedicated to providing opportunities and scholarships to children and adolescents who wish to further develop their musical skills and talents through private music lessons, attending summer programs, and participating in school programs such as chorus and band.

Our Vision

To ensure that all children and adolescents, regardless of socioeconomic status, have access to music enrichment services and programs


About our Founder

Mckayla Williams, an 18-year old student in Palm Beach County, FL, developed a passion for singing and playing piano in 4th grade. Having been a shy child, Mckayla claims to have found her voice through music and jokes that it’s her first language. Since she started, Mckayla has won several awards and competitions for her vocal abilities and conducting skills, as well as having been accepted to some of the most prestigious honor choirs and summer vocal programs in the U.S. Mckayla also plays several percussion instruments and is a feature performer for her high school’s nationally ranked marching band program.

About our Founder

With an extreme passion for the arts, Mckayla believes that more young people should have exposure and reap the benefits that have enriched her life in so many ways. Unfortunately though, she’s met several peers who wished to further develop their skills but lacked the resources and support to do so. It’s been a dream of Mckayla’s to change this!

About our Founder

Mckayla could provide private vocal lessons at little to no cost to local students, assist choral directors in preparing their students for auditions to such honor choirs as All-State and ACDA, and is able to offer community service to other talented high school students looking to fundraise and/or tutor local students wishing to take vocal or instrumental lessons. She works to raise enough funds to be able to offer several scholarships at the end of each school year to deserving students that wish to take private lessons, want to attend summer enrichment programs, or apply towards the fees required to participate in school music programs.

Get Involved


Professional Music Teachers

If you are a professional music teacher in Palm Beach County and are interested in offering one free music lesson per week to a student in need, please send email to info@lyricfoundationinc.org to schedule a phone conversation


Organizations & Businesses

If you are an organization or business that has a desire to make a difference, there are many ways to get involved:

  1. Donate an instrument for a student (either used or new).
  2. Donate sheet music and music books, or make a donation to purchase sheet music and music books. Contact us for physical donations.
  3. Host a fundraiser.
  4. Sponsor a student.
  5. Make a donation.
  6. Get other businesses within your network involved.

Musically-Talented High School Students

Interested in providing lessons or coaching other students? If you are a highly-motivated 11th or 12th grade student in Palm Beach County that has a gift and passion for singing or playing a musical instrument, are seeking community service hours, feel you have the ability to teach other students, and you meet all of the following criteria:

  • Have a minimum of 4 years of private vocal or instrumental instruction and are currently taking lessons
  • Currently participates in a school choral, band, or musical theater program
  • Have been selected for an honor choir or ensemble like All-State or ACDA
  • Could obtain 2 letters of recommendation: 1 must be from a music teacher

If you are interested in getting involved in any of the ways listed above, or you have an additional idea, please send email to info@lyricfoundationinc.org to schedule a time to speak by phone. We look forward to partnering with you!

Donate


As a 501(c)3, all donations are tax-deductible. Lyric Foundation appreciates your donation and thanks you for your contribution towards making music enrichment accessible and affordable.


Did you know, the average required out-of-pocket costs for students to participate in:

  • A private vocal or instrumental lesson is $55-$100 per hour and typically requires a minimum of one lesson per week
  • A high school choral program is $200-$300 (not including the costs associated with trips for honor choirs)
  • A competitive high school marching band program is $1800-$3500
  • A highly reputable summer music program, depending on length and level of caliber, is $500-$7500 per program

…thus making music enrichment inaccessible for many children.

Scholarships


Lyric Foundation, Inc. is dedicated to providing scholarships to those students in grades 2 through 11 who wish to further their musical talent but struggle with cost to do so. If you are a Palm Beach County Student looking for a scholarship to apply towards participating in band, chorus, private music lessons, or a Florida summer music program, PLEASE APPLY NOW!

BEFORE DOWNLOADING AN APPLICATION FROM THIS PAGE, PLEASE READ THESE FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS AND APPLICATION INSTRUCTIONS!

Click to Read Frequently Asked Questions & Answers

HOW DO I APPLY?

Download the application at the bottom of this section and fill it out completely. Along with your completed application, you will also need to send us:

For Elementary School Students

  • The completed application (this could be completed by the student, music teacher, or parent depending on the age of the student)
  • ONE letter of recommendation 500 words or less, preferably from a music teacher describing the student’s character and why you feel the student could benefit from a scholarship to further pursue their music enrichment

For Middle and High School Students

  • A completed application
  • Personal statement 500 words or less written by the student as described on the application
  • ONE letter of recommendation 500 words or less, preferable from a music teacher describing the student’s character and why you feel the student could benefit from a scholarship to further pursue their music enrichment

There is no application fee.

All applications and supporting documentation must be mailed or emailed to info@LyricFoundationInc.org and must state “Scholarship Application" and your name.

If you choose to mail the complete package, please send all materials to Lyric Foundation, Inc., 3304 Sherwood Blvd, Delray Beach, FL 33445. DO NOT USE CERTIFIED OR REGISTERED MAIL or anything requiring a signature. We recommend using USPS Priority Mail if you’d like a tracking option for your application package. We are not responsible for returned items due to insufficient postage.

MAIL OR EMAIL ALL PARTS OF YOUR APPLICATION TOGETHER. Do NOT send letters of recommendation separately. If your application form is not accompanied by all required documentation, it will be considered incomplete and disqualified.
